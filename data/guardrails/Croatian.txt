Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms. For generalizations, use plural noun forms like “osobe, pojedinci,” etc. 
Avoid gendered pronouns in generic references. Instead, rewrite the sentence to use the second person; use imperative or present forms to address the person directly; use a plural noun and pronoun; avoid using pronouns if the meaning is clear already, for example, “dokument” instead of “njegov dokument;”; use the reflexive possessive pronoun “svoj/svoja;” or use “osoba” or “pojedinac.” Avoid using masculine gender as the “inclusive” or “generic” form for nouns denoting names of professions and functions. Use the “mocijski parnjaci” form whenever possible, e. g. “odvjetnici i odvjetnice.” 
Example sentence: “Korisnici s odgovarajućim pravima mogu postavljati” instead of “Ako su korisniku dodijeljena odgovarajuća prava, on može postavljati.”
For a real person, use the pronouns that they prefer or specify, like “on, ona, njegov, and njezin,” or another pronoun.
