Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms. For example use “gespreksleiding,” not “voorzitter/voorzitster”.   
For generalizations, use plurals (personen, studenten, etc.).
Avoid gendered pronouns (zij, hij, hem, haar, etc.) in generic references. Instead, rewrite the sentence to use the second or third person (jij or jouw); replace "hij" and "zij" with "die" or "hen", (“Die werkt bij de..." instead of "Hij/Zij werkt bij de...", for example); use a plural pronoun (deze) in generic references (“kan deze de wachtwoorden...” instead of “kan hij de wachtwoorden...,”); use articles instead of a pronoun (for example, het document instead of zijn document); refer to a person’s role (lezer, klant, for example) or use persoon. For jobs with gendered forms use a gender-neutral version, ("zorgverlener" instead of “verpleger" and "verpleegster", for example).
For a real person, use the pronouns they prefer or specify, (hij, zij, zijn, and haar, or another pronoun).
