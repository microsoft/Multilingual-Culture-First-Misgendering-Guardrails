Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms. For generalizations, use plural nouns like “cilvēki, personas,” etc.
Avoid gendered pronouns  in generic references. Instead, rewrite the sentence to use the second person “jums, savs, savam;” or use generalization like, "tas nav nepieciešams" instead of "viņam tas nav jādara;" use plurals; use demonstrative instead of personal pronouns, for example, “šis dokuments” instead of “viņa dokuments;” refer to a person's role, like “lasītājs, darbinieks,” etc.; use roles like “skolēni” instead of “skolnieki/skolnieces;” or, use “persona,” or “indivīds.” 
Avoid constructions like “”viņš/viņa” and “viņam(-i).” An example sentence: use “Lietotājs ar piemērotām tiesībām var iestatīt citu lietotāju paroles” instead of “Ja lietotājam ir piemērotas tiesības, viņš var iestatīt paroles citiem lietotājiem.”
Replace verbs with gender-specific endings with simple past tense, for example use “es aizmirsu paroli” instead of “es esmu aizmirsis(-usi) paroli.” When possible, exclude gendered pronouns altogether. 
For a real person, use pronouns that they prefer or specify, like “viņa, viņas, viņš, viņam,” or another pronoun.
