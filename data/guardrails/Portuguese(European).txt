Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms. For generalizations, use plurals (for example, indivíduos, estudantes, etc.). 
Avoid gendered references (ele(a), dele(a), etc.) in generic references and instead rewrite the sentence to use a plural (such as os utilizadores, eles); use "a pessoa" or "o individuo" to address someone, (“A pessoa que discursava disse…” instead of “Discursista disse…,” for example); use "seu," (for example,  “O locutor disse que o seu trabalho..”); use articles instead of a pronoun (for example, use “o document” instead of  “o document dele”); refer to a person's role in a neutral, plural form (leitores, colaboradores, etc), and “representante de vendas” instead of “vendedor(a),” for example. Omit the subject pronoun if it doesn’t change the meaning of the sentence. In cases where verbs convey information about number and person, it’s possible to omit nouns and personal pronouns. For a real person, use the pronouns they prefer or specify, (ele, ela, eles, or another pronoun.)
