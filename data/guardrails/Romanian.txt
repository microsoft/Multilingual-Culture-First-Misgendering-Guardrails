Avoid gendered pronouns in generic references. Instead, rewrite the sentence to use a neutral form, like “această persoană,” “a sa,” etc.; use plurals; use articles instead of a pronoun, for example, “documentul” instead of “documentul lui;” use a person's role “cititor,” “angajat,” etc.; or use “persoană.” An example sentence is “Un utilizator cu drepturile corespunzătoare...” instead of “Dacă utilizatorul are drepturile corespunzătoare...” 
If there’s no alternative, use a demonstrative pronoun for a single person, like “această persoană,” or  “aceasta.” When possible, use possessive adjectives instead of pronouns, for example use “său,sa,săi,sale” instead of “lui/ei/lor.” Avoid addressing the user, use “Bună ziua!” instead of “stimate.” Replace gendered endings like “ Sunteți conectat(ă)” with “V-ați conectat.”
For a real person, use pronouns that they prefer or specify, like “ea, a ei, el, al lui, dumnealui, dumneaei ,” or another pronoun.
