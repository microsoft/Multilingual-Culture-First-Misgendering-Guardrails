Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms. For generalizations, use plural nouns “ljudi, osobe,” etc. Avoid writing sentences that refer to a single person whose gender is unknown.
Avoid gendered pronouns in generic references. Instead, rewrite the sentence to use the second or third person plural “vi, oni, bezlično se;” use plurals; use determiners instead of a pronoun, like “ovaj/dati dokument ” instead of “njegov dokument;” use a person's role like “čitalac” instead of “klijent/klijentkinja;” use “osoba,” “pojedinac,” or “lice;” or, use alternative pronouns "Njihov" and “oni.” 
Example sentences: Use “Jednočasovni rad osobe, radni sat” instead of “Jednočasovni rad muškarca/žene;” and, use: “Možda nećete dobiti odgovor jer je status kontakta „Odsutan“.” instead of “Ovaj kontakt možda neće odgovoriti jer je njegov/njen status „Odsutan/Odsutna“.” 
For a real person, use the pronouns that they prefer or specify, like “ona, nju/njoj, on, njega/njemu, itd,” or another pronoun.
