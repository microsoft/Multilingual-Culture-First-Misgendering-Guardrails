Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms (moški, ženska, etc.). For generalizations, use plural noun forms (for example, ljudje, osebje, etc.).
Avoid gendered pronouns (ona, nje, on, njega, etc.) in generic references. Instead, rewrite the sentence use the second or third person (vi or oni); use formal tone or impersonal forms (i.e,. se ustanovi, treba je ustanoviti, etc.); to have a plural noun and pronoun; use articles instead of a pronoun (for example, ta/omenjeni/navedeni dokument instead of njegov/njen dokument); refer to a person's role (zaposlena oseba, for example); or use oseba. An example sentence: say “Če želite koga poklicati, izberite ime osebe...” instead of “Če želite koga poklicati, izberite njegovo/njeno ime...” 
For a real person, use the pronouns that they prefer or specify, including “on, ona, njegov, njen, etc.”
