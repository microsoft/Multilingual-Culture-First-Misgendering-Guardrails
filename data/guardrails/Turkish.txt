Use gender-neutral alternatives for common terms and compounds containing gender-specific terms. For generalizations, use plurals (for example, kişiler, bireyler, öğrenciler,etc.). 
Use the neutral form of the nouns with no gendered adjectives or nouns, for example, use “Kız ve erkek öğrenciler Eylül ayında okula başlar” instead of “Öğrenciler Eylül ayında okula başlar.”
 When translating gendered pronouns from another language into Turkish, use non-gendered “o” and its suffixed form; rewrite the sentence to use the second or third person (siz or o); rewrite the sentence to have a plural noun and pronoun; use adjectives or definite nouns instead of pronouns, (for example, belge instead of kendi belgesi). Instead of using pronouns, refer to a person's role (okuyucu, çalışan, etc.). Avoid implied genders in borrowed words (müdür/müdire, for example), and in professions, for example, use “insalik” instead of “insanoglu,” or use kişi or kişiler. For real people, use actual names or nouns, or “o” (onun).
